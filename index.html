Iméa

Iméa is the god of cold and winter. She is generally portrayed as a giantess with white skin, pale blue eyes, and white hair, wearing wolf furs. As the goddess of cold and winter, the people of Endon see her influence in all powerful weather. It is said her breath brings the biting winter gales, her temperament brings the chilling cold, and her anger brings the feared rogue blasts.

Oldroar

Oldroar is the ancient god of knowledge. He is associated with natural and magical studies. Oldroar is often depicted as a huge, fearsome iron dragon with blackened scales. However, illustrations of dragons are inconsistent, and it is unclear if anyone has ever actually seen one.

Jelenwa

Jelenwa is the goddess of bounty and kindness. Farmers, hunters, and gatherers pray to her for bountiful harvests and successful hunts. Expectant mothers pray to Jelenwa for healthy babies, while hopeful fathers pray for fertility. Her image varies depending on the culture and race of the believer, usually appearing as an idealized beauty of that race.

Yemop Lightcollar

Yemop is the god of curiosity and is extremely popular among the halflings of Endon. He is depicted as a halfling boy with a mop of curly red hair, always wearing a bright white trench coat with silver or gold embroideries. The first day of the stretch of Yemop is celebrated in halfling settlements with song and feasting. On this day, the most popular halfling is chosen to be "Yemop for the day" and wears a replica of the trenchcoat. They are showered with gifts like scrolls, books, and writing tools, all of which are donated to the settlement's school or library at the end of the day to inspire curiosity in the next generation.

Petpetor

Petpetor is the playful and mischievous god of the Harengon, presiding over the natural flow of things and the happiness of life. His followers visualize him as a finely dressed Harengon male who is quick to smile. It is said that purple flowers grow in his path as he wanders the land playing tricks on poor souls.

Kuzrol

Kuzrol is the goddess of death and is almost universally feared. Her image is that of an amorphous female form made of black oil or water that drips from her fingertips. It is believed that where these black drips fall, death follows. Consequently, spilling blackened oil or ink is considered very unlucky in much of Endon. Her followers, mostly outcasts, tend to acknowledge the natural order of beginnings and ends, often living austere lifestyles and and being associated with dark magics.

Gibbix

Gibbix is the god of fortunes, named after the brightest star in the sky which twinkles gold and red. While he governs luck, he is also the embodiment of wealth, avarice, and deceit. In most of Endon, trade is done through bartering; physical currency is a rarity, hoarded by the wealthy elite in the largest cities. Because of this association with the cruelties of wealth, common folk believe that fortune is the ultimate trick. Gibbix is therefore viewed with deep suspicion—a gambler might pray for his favor, but they do so knowing that a sudden windfall is a dangerous gift.

Humdizal

Humdizal is the god of strategies. He is the primary god of Orcs, Goblins, Kobolds, and Giants. The more warlike half-orcs of Endon are also known to worship him. He is often depicted as a set of animated, gleaming full plate armor wielding a double-bladed battle axe.

The Calendar of Endon

The calendar of Endon is known as “The Coil.” While often drawn as a simple chart for practical use, its name reflects the common belief that a year is a cycle of hardship and renewal to be endured—a mortal coil. It is divided into 8 "stretches," each 30 days long. Endon only has two seasons. The first day of each stretch is celebrated in honor of the god it is named after, if that god is recognized in the region.

The Cold

The first four stretches make up the coldest half of the year. The second and third stretches are the most frigid.

The Wind

The last four stretches are marked by a constant, gentle east-to-west wind. These stretches are warmer, but the a windchill can make it feel worse than The Cold. During this time, parts of Endon may experience tornadoes and sudden, dangerous wind gusts known as “Rogue blasts." While a natural phenomenon, these sudden walls of air are widely believed to be fits of Iméa’s temper, making them especially feared.

The World of Endon

An Overview of the World

The known world is called Endon. The flow of information is slow and political borders are not well defined, meaning most people have a limited understanding of the world's full scope.

The Major Landmasses

The peoples of Endon use simple, descriptive names for the major landmasses, as no single group has enough influence to name a continent.

  • The Central Continent: The largest and most central landmass.
  • The Eastern Continent: A long continent to the east.
  • The Southern Continent: An island-continent to the south.
  • The Islands: A chain of islands in the southwest, known only to seafarers and the most well-traveled scholars.

Regions of Endon

The Central Continent

The Central Continent is the ancestral home of the dwarves. They have established a formal kingdom, though their influence rarely extends beyond the great central mountain range and its immediate foothills. The Dwarven King, Kildrak Ironbuckle, rules from the capitol city of The Alabaster Peak and has established a series of baronies dotted along the length of the mountain range to protect his domain.

Far Lake

Far Lake and the surrounding forests of the Lowlands are home to the largest population of wood elves on the continent. While primarily wood elves, some tribes have introduced high elves and dark elves to the region, creating a diverse elven society.

Their society is tribal, composed of numerous self-governing tribes. They are skilled hunter-gatherers who live in harmony with the dense forests. The elves are also renowned artisans, known for producing intricate wooden sculptures, fine pelts, and sturdy leathers which they occasionally trade.

The elves of Far Lake are fiercely independent and do not permit other races to settle permanently in their villages. Despite this isolationist nature, they are not hostile. To avoid unnecessary conflict, they treat unexpected visitors with grace and hospitality before sending them on their way.

Their primary deity is Jelenwa, the goddess of bounty. In addition to the main pantheon, the Far Lake tribes also venerate an ancient warrior hero named Elenoth, whom they worship as a demigod.

Lone Mountain

This solitary peak is believed by the elves to be the home of Kuzrol, the goddess of death. Due to this dark association, Lone Mountain is feared and universally avoided by all elvenkind. Other races, however, do not share this certainty. Most have no specific location for the goddess, while some believe she inhabits a lightless underworld, and others claim she resides in the vast frozen desert that covers the western part of the continent.

The Alabaster Peak

The largest and tallest mountain in the central range, Alabaster Peak is the capitol of the Dwarven Kingdom, home to King Kildrak Ironbuckle.

Baron's Bluff

The southernmost Barony of the Dwarven Kingdom, Baron's Bluff is a small settlement with a budding iron mine, ruled by Baron Ragnar Oakshield. Despite its small population, it holds a position of significant diplomatic importance. Due to its proximity to the port of Lebno and the human-governed city of Chaptain, it serves as the primary point of contact between the reclusive Dwarven Kingdom and the other major races of the region, including the Harengon, Halflings, Elves, and Humans.

Lebno

Lebno is a small port town on the southern coast of the Central Continent, a place that can be bustling with activity one moment and completely desolate the next. Though the human city of Chaptain claims dominion over it, they rarely enforce their laws here, leaving the town to its own devices. Lebno is known for the nearly ever-present, wispy fog that permeates the town and surrounding waters in the early morning.

The port itself consists of a single dock made of heavy wooden timber, a few scattered farm plots, and the town's main hub: a tavern called "The Mist's Milk." The tavern is somewhat famous for its "forever stew"—a mix of potatoes, carrots, venison, and whatever herbs and spices were available "that day"—prepared in a prized, giant iron pot that is kept over a continuously fed fire. It is one of the few places outside of a major city where both barter and currency are accepted for goods and services.

The proprietor of "The Mist's Milk" is Milo, a Harengon from Bodán. She operates the tavern on behalf of its human-recognized owner, Task Master Turdor, sending him regular quotas of trade goods and Dwarven-minted iron coins. Over the last half-generation, Milo has seen everything from wanted men slipping through the port to visits from the local Baron from Baron's Bluff and the Harengon Family Council leader. She is hard to surprise, quick-witted, and a natural survivor.

Chaptain

Chaptain is a human-governed city that exerts its influence over the surrounding region, including the port of Lebno. Within its walls, human society dominates, but the city is surrounded by sprawling burroughs inhabited by a subjugated Halfling population. The humans of Chaptain view Halflings as lesser beings, fit only for menial labor. This oppressive social structure, however, blinds the human leadership to the complex and hidden culture that thrives within the Halfling community.

The Eastern Continent

The eastern continent contains a vast delta of ice-cold wetland swamps. The people that live there are the Caliopans, who call their lands Caliopis and their language The Speech.

Caliopis

The Caliopans are a tribal society of Centaurs governed by a gerontocracy. They have no concept of currency or wealth, living off the land and prizing strength, accomplishment, and merit above all else. They are almost unheard of outside of their native lands and have no contact with other peoples or other races.

Bodán

Located on the Southern Continent, Bodán is the great capitol of the Harengon. It maintains a crucial link to the Central Continent through an established sea trade route with the port of Lebno. The Harengon's patron deity is Petpetor.

Important Persons

Bullion Goldtooth

The treasurer and secretary of Baron's Bluff, Bullion is the right-hand dwarf to Baron Ragnar Oakshield. He handles most of the Baron's day-to-day administrative tasks, freeing up his superior for personal endeavors.

Elenoth

An ancient warrior hero of the wood elves of Far Lake. Elenoth is venerated by the Far Lake tribe as a demigod, a figure of great reverence alongside their primary deity, Jelenwa.

Kildrak Ironbuckle

The King of the Dwarves. Kildrak Ironbuckle rules his entire kingdom from the capitol city of The Alabaster Peak, overseeing the various baronies established along the central mountain range.

Milo

A Harengon from Bodán, Milo is the proprietor of "The Mist's Milk" tavern in the port town of Lebno. While she operates the tavern on behalf of Task Master Turdor, she is fiercely independent and could arrange passage back to her homeland if she chose. She remains in Lebno because she enjoys the excitement of her work, managing the tavern's affairs with little interference so long as she meets her quotas. A quick-witted and unflappable survivor, she has tended the tavern for half a generation, seeing all manner of folk pass through.

Ragnar Oakshield

The Baron of Baron's Bluff. As the leader of the southernmost dwarven barony, Ragnar holds a critical role in the kingdom's diplomatic relations with the outside world.

Task Master Turdor

A cruel, fat-bellied official from the human town of Chaptain with a strange physique of a large gut and spindly limbs. As Task Master, he is the human-recognized owner of "The Mist's Milk" tavern in Lebno and often represents the interests of Chaptain's Mayor in dealings with the dwarves of Baron's Bluff. He lives with his turgid, bitter wife and two young sons on an expansive potato farm in the Halfling burroughs just outside the city walls.

Seas and Seafaring

Most boat travel takes place on freshwater rivers and lakes, or cautiously along the coastlines of the continents. True open-sea travel is exceptionally rare, and the continents are largely isolated from one another. Only a few established trade routes exist for crossing the seas. The most significant of these connects the Central Continent to the Southern Continent, running from the tiny port village of Lebno to the great Harengon capitol of Bodán.

Languages of the People

  • Caliopan: The language spoken by the centaurs of Caliopis. While outsiders refer to it as Caliopan, natives call it "The Speech" or "De Speech." The Caliopans who have encountered other races believe them to be degenerate in some way, and consider their languages improper forms of speech.

Climate and Geography

The world of Endon is overwhelmingly cold. Most regions see snow and ice for much of the year, and survival is a constant struggle against the freezing temperatures. There are a few rare exceptions, small pockets of the world that can reach sub-tropical temperatures during the peak of the warmest days, but these are few and far between.

The Central Continent is a vast tundra. A great frozen desert of ice and rock covers much of its western expanse. A more temperate region known as the "Lowlands" exists in the south, a sweeping territory that runs from Far Lake, along the southern coast past Baron's Bluff and Lebno, and connects to the settlement of Chaptain in the west.
